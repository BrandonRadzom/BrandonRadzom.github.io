About Me

I am a 3rd year Astronomy PhD candidate at Indiana University (IU) with a minor in Scientific Computing. I work with Dr. Songhu Wang on several projects related to short-period gas giant formation. Particularly, I am interested in using N-body simulations to study dynamical origins of hot and warm Jupiters in multi-planet systems. Additionally, I am heavily involved in outreach efforts at IU, serving as the Undergraduate Research Coordinator for the Astronomy Department, writing for ScIU (IU's graduate science blog), and regularly volunteering at local events.

I earned a B.S. in Astronomy-Physics, a B.S. in Physics and a certificate in Computer Sciences from the University of Wisconsin - Madison in May of 2020. During my undergraduate career, I also participated in astronomy and physics outreach often as the Volunteer Coordinator and Vice President of the Astronomy Club.

When not doing science, I am likely on my feet. First and foremost, I am an avid long distance runner of 10 years. These days, I run anything from a 5ks to full marathons, but my current focus is to make it out to the Boston marathon in 2024. I also love a good hike anywhere out in nature; anything with lakes, rivers and mountains are a plus! Originating from Minnesota, I try to make the most of my winters by cross country skiing.
