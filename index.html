About Me

I am a 2nd year Astronomy PhD student at Indiana University, and have obtained a minor in Scientific Computing. I work with Dr. Songhu Wang on several computational projects relating to the stability of exoplanetary systems. Particularly, I am interested in using N-body simulations to study fundamental limits of planetary growth in high-mass, high-multiplicity and highly compact systems, as well as the best statistics to characterize gravitational instability multi-planet systems generally. I earned a B.S. in Astronomy-Physics, a B.S. in Physics and a certificate in Computer Sciences from the University of Wisconsin - Madison in May of 2020. During my undergraduate career, I was heavily involved in astronomy and physics outreach as the Vice President of the Astronomy Club.

When not studying, researching or doing public outreach, I am likely on my feet. Firstly, I am an avid long distance runner of 10 years. These days, I run anything from a 5ks to full marathons and even qualified for and ran the 2020 virtual Boston marathon. I also love a good hike anywhere out in nature though lakes, rivers and mountains are a plus! Being from Minnesota, I try to make the most of my winters by cross country skiing at local parks.
